FuelWise — Figma Revision Prompt (addendum to v1)
Apply the following revisions to the existing screen designs.

Screen 2 — Getting Started: Energy Level Selector
Replace the five horizontal segment bar with two conditional visualizations based on the detected fuel type.
For gas vehicles, display a circular gauge illustration styled after a car dashboard fuel gauge. The gauge is a semicircle arc spanning from E on the left to F on the right. Tick marks sit along the arc at equal intervals. A needle rotates to indicate the current level. The E label and the needle fill color use F15025 when the level is in the lower quarter. The gauge face is FFFFFF, the outer ring is 191919, and all labels use 191919. Below the gauge, five tap targets labeled E, 1/4, 1/2, 3/4, and F allow the user to position the needle. The selected tap target label turns F15025.
For electric vehicles, display a horizontal battery bar in place of the gauge. The battery icon is the standard rectangular body with a small positive terminal nub on the right. The fill progresses left to right. The filled portion uses F15025. The unfilled portion uses E6E8E6. The battery outline is 191919 at 1.5px. Five tap targets below the battery labeled 0, 25, 50, 75, and 100 with a percent sign set the fill level. The selected tap target turns F15025. A small label above the battery reads the selected percentage in 14px 191919 medium.
Both variants retain the caption "This helps us estimate your range" in 12px CED0CE below the selector.

Screen 8 — Profile: Add Vehicle Navigation
The circular plus button next to "My vehicles" now routes the user back to Screen 2, the Getting Started vehicle registration flow. In Figma, connect the plus button tap target to the Screen 2 frame using a Navigate To interaction with a slide-up transition. A back arrow appears in the top left of Screen 2 when accessed from Profile, allowing the user to return to Profile without completing the full onboarding. The back arrow is 191919 and tapping it navigates back to Screen 8 with a slide-down dismiss transition.

Screens 4 and 5 — Nearest and Cheapest: Store Filters
Directly below the search bar and range input, add a horizontally scrollable row of filter pills. The row has no visible scroll indicator. Each pill is 32px tall, has 12px horizontal padding, 999px border radius, E6E8E6 background, CED0CE border at 0.5px, and 191919 text at 13px. When a filter pill is active, it fills with F15025 and text becomes FFFFFF. The pills read: All, Shell, Chevron, BP, Costco, Sam's Club, Tesla, ChargePoint, 24 Hours, Car Wash. The Costco and Sam's Club pills, when tapped while the user has no saved membership, show a small inline alert below the filter row in 12px F15025 reading "Membership required — add one in your profile." Each station result card in the list below now has a star icon on the far right of the card. The star is outlined CED0CE when unfavorited. When tapped, it fills solid F15025 and the station is added to the Favorite Locations list in the Profile screen. A brief confirmation label fades in and out below the card reading "Added to favorites" in 12px F15025.

Screens 4 and 5 — Any Listed Station: Favorite Star
Wherever stations appear throughout the app including in trip stop lists and search results, a star icon sits on the far right of the row or card. Outlined CED0CE when not saved, solid F15025 when favorited. Tapping toggles the state. This star appears on every station surface consistently.

Screen 6 — Trip Planner: AI-Assisted Mode Flow
When the user selects the AI-assisted card and taps Start Trip, do not navigate directly to the active route. Instead, transition to a full-screen loading state. The background is FFFFFF. Centered on the screen is a pulsing ring animation in F15025. Below the ring, text reads "Planning your route" in 16px 191919 medium. A subline in 14px CED0CE reads "Calculating stops based on your current range." This screen holds for 2 to 3 seconds then transitions to an AI Route Preview screen.
The AI Route Preview screen shows the full planned route. The upper half of the screen is the map with the F15025 route line drawn end to end and labeled stop markers numbered 1 through however many stops were calculated. Each marker is a circle with F15025 fill and FFFFFF number inside. Below the map, a scrollable summary panel lists each stop as a card. Each stop card shows the stop number, station name, miles from previous stop, estimated fuel level on arrival shown as a mini gauge or battery bar matching the vehicle type, price per gallon or kWh, and a favorite star. At the bottom of the summary panel, two full-width buttons are stacked. The top button reads "Accept and navigate" with F15025 background and FFFFFF text. The bottom button reads "Replan route" with FFFFFF background, CED0CE border, and 191919 text. Tapping Accept and navigate transitions to Screen 7, the active navigation view.

Screen 7 — Trip In Action: Bottom Navigation Bar
Add a persistent bottom dashboard bar to the active navigation screen. The bar sits above the bottom safe area and has a FFFFFF background with a CED0CE top border at 0.5px and 16px padding. The bar contains four icon-and-label tap targets: Route (shows the stop list panel sliding up from the bottom), Stops (jumps to the next stop detail), Fuel (opens a quick fuel level update modal), and Exit (presents a confirmation dialog reading "End this trip?" with Confirm in F15025 and Cancel in 191919). Active state for the current tab uses F15025 for both icon and label. Inactive tabs use CED0CE. This bar ensures the user can always exit navigation or access key controls without being locked into the screen.

Screen 6 — Trip Planner: Manual Mode Flow
When the user selects the Manual card and taps Start Trip, transition directly to the manual trip navigation screen. This screen is split into two sections. The upper two-thirds shows the live map with the route drawn in F15025. As the user drives, their position dot moves along the route. Gas stations within a reasonable corridor of the route are shown as map pins using a smaller version of the station pin, filled E6E8E6 with a 191919 fuel pump icon inside. Tapping any station pin brings up a compact bottom sheet that slides up. The bottom sheet shows the station name in 16px 191919 medium, distance off route in 12px CED0CE, current price in F15025 at 18px medium, available fuel grades as pills, hours of operation in 12px CED0CE, and a favorite star in the top right corner. Two buttons sit at the bottom of the sheet: "Add as stop" in F15025 background with FFFFFF text, which inserts the station into the active route and redraws the map, and "Dismiss" in FFFFFF background with CED0CE border and 191919 text.
The lower third of the manual navigation screen is a persistent panel with FFFFFF background and CED0CE top border. The panel shows a label "Stations along your route" in 14px 191919 medium and a horizontally scrollable row of compact station cards. Each card is 120px wide, E6E8E6 background, CED0CE border, 12px radius, and shows the station name truncated to one line, distance in miles in 12px CED0CE, price in F15025, and a plus icon to add it as a stop. When added, the plus turns to a checkmark in F15025 and the station pin on the map updates to a filled F15025 pin indicating it is now a planned stop.
The same bottom navigation bar from the AI flow applies here, with the same four tabs: Route, Stops, Fuel, and Exit.
